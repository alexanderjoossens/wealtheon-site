Growth — investing in the companies of tomorrow

The Growth strategy invests in innovative market leaders, often in sectors such as technology and healthcare, that drive their businesses with new products, services, and technologies. These companies reinvest profits into further growth rather than paying out dividends, and typically trade at higher price-to-earnings multiples that reflect their future prospects.

Investment philosophy

  • Innovative companies with distinctive products, services, or technologies, often in technology and healthcare
  • Reinvestment of profits — less focus on dividends, more on compounding future growth
  • Higher P/E valuations, justified by strong forward growth expectations

Our approach

A top-down process: we start from the macro-economic environment and global trends to identify promising regions and sectors, then build an actively managed portfolio. We anticipate economic cycles and adjust positions and liquidity to capture opportunities and manage volatility. Equities are quality businesses with strong balance sheets, experienced management, and low debt — large caps (market cap above €10bn), with a preference for technology-driven sector leaders, complemented by thematic trackers. Bond exposure is primarily investment-grade, diversified through ETFs and bond funds.

Risk considerations

Risk profile 2-6. Growth shares offer attractive opportunities but can show meaningful price swings (volatility) and are sensitive to broad market declines (market risk). The strategy carries a relatively high allocation to the technology and healthcare sectors (sector concentration).

Value — investing in intrinsic worth

The Value strategy seeks underappreciated companies with genuine growth potential whose shares trade at lower valuation multiples than the market. The focus is on intrinsic worth: a sound business, capable management, and a strong financial balance sheet. The discount to fair value provides a margin of safety and a long-term horizon over which that value can be recognised.

Investment philosophy

  • Value creation — a focus on underappreciated companies with growth potential
  • Valuation — our value holdings typically trade at lower price multiples
  • Strong fundamentals — the business, the management, and the balance sheet

Our approach

A bottom-up process: we search for individual companies worldwide. We follow macro-economic developments, but they do not drive our choices. The portfolio is actively managed — selecting individual companies alongside a small number of exclusive external niche funds — and is contrarian by nature, meaning it can deviate from the market consensus. We grow together with the value created by and within the companies we hold.

Risk considerations

Risk profile 5-7. The strategy is built for the long term: returns come as the underlying businesses create value over time. Because the approach is contrarian, the portfolio can look different from, and move differently to, the broad market consensus.

High Conviction — exceptional growth potential

High Conviction is Wealtheon's most concentrated, technology-driven strategy: a limited selection of individual companies with exceptional growth potential. We apply a buy-and-hold principle to strong performers in the technology and medtech sectors, aiming for a higher return expectation through genuine concentration rather than broad diversification.

Investment philosophy

  • Disruptive — companies that break existing market structures, pressure incumbents, and create new business models
  • Investing in the future — businesses investing heavily in R&D across robotics, artificial intelligence, cloud computing, and digital payments
  • Strategic edge — a unique market position relative to direct competitors

Our approach

A bottom-up, buy-and-hold approach applied to a small selection of individual, technology-driven stocks, focused on the United States and deliberately highly concentrated. The strategy is actively managed: Wealtheon works with the independent firm Financiële Diensten Amsterdam (FDA).

Asset allocation

RegionsUnited States 73% · Europe 27%
Top sectorsTechnology 45% · Health & pharma 19% · Consumer discretionary 8% · Financials 8% · Industrials 7%

Risk considerations

Risk profile 7. The concentrated nature of the portfolio means higher risk from limited diversification (concentration risk). It rewards "time in the market": staying fully invested, accepting market volatility, and not being driven by short-term emotion.

Financial Intermediaries

We work closely with independent financial advisers, family offices, wealth managers, and other financial intermediaries who seek a trusted investment management partner for their clients. Our intermediary model is built on genuine partnership, transparency, and aligned interests.

As an independent asset manager with no proprietary products beyond our own strategies, Wealtheon is free of the conflicts of interest that affect larger, bank-affiliated managers. We manage your clients' assets the same way we manage our own — with patience, rigour, and a long-term perspective.

Dedicated Support

Each intermediary partner is assigned a dedicated relationship manager at Wealtheon, providing a single point of contact for all operational, reporting, and investment matters.

Full Transparency

We provide complete transparency on portfolio composition, transaction history, performance attribution, and fee structures — enabling you to fulfil your own reporting obligations with ease.

White-Label Options

For qualifying intermediary partners, we can structure mandates and reporting under your branding, allowing you to deliver Wealtheon's investment quality within your own client relationships.

Co-Investment

Consistent with our principle of aligned interests, Wealtheon staff co-invest in the strategies we manage — a meaningful demonstration of confidence in our own process.

Research Access

Intermediary partners have access to our in-house research, market views, and strategy updates — giving you deeper insight to share with your own clients.

Flexible Structures

We accommodate direct discretionary mandates, fund investments, and hybrid arrangements — whatever structure best suits your clients' legal, tax, and operational requirements.
